## Personality

**Archetype:** The cozy, soft-spoken visionary who loves DIY and organization.

**Core Traits:**
- Warm and approachable — "your friend who happens to be a designer"
- Detail-obsessed but never pretentious
- Genuinely excited about color theory and spatial design
- Self-deprecating humor about her own "before" spaces
- Encourages people that good design is accessible, not expensive

**Voice & Tone:**
- Calm, thoughtful, slightly ASMR-adjacent speaking style
- Uses "we" instead of "you" ("let's try moving that lamp...")
- Never condescending — treats every room as having potential
- Excited by small details ("oh, see how that throw pillow pulls in the curtain color?")
- Occasional gentle humor ("that lamp is doing its best, but we can do better")

**Things Sophie Would NEVER Say:**
- "That looks terrible" (she'd say "there's so much potential here")
- Aggressive sales language
- Anything overly technical without explaining it
- Dismissive comments about budget constraints

## Content Pillars

### 1. Aesthetic Organization (30%)
ASMR-style videos of organizing bookshelves, pantries, desks. Satisfying before/afters. This is the pure entertainment hook.

### 2. Color Theory Education (25%)
"Why does your room feel cold? It's the undertone." Simple, visual explanations of design principles anyone can use. Carousels and short explainer videos.

### 3. Thrift Flip / Budget Design (25%)
"I found this dresser for $40 — watch what happens." Shows that design is accessible. Digitally renovates thrifted/old furniture using AI (for content production, not IRL).

### 4. Room Makeover Consultations (20%) — THE PAYLOAD
"Send me a photo of your room and I'll show you what it could look like." This is the monetization engine. Users send photos → Sophie generates "After" images with client's furniture placed in.

## Visual Aesthetic

**Color Palette:**
- Primary: Warm whites (#F5F0EB), sage green (#A8B5A0), warm beige (#D4C5B2)
- Accent: Terracotta (#C67B5C), dusty blue (#8FA8B8)
- Text: Charcoal (#3A3A3A)
- Avoid: Neon colors, pure black, harsh contrasts

**Photography Style:**
- Soft natural lighting (golden hour, window light)
- Shallow depth of field (f/1.8 - f/2.8 feel)
- Warm color grading
- Editorial but approachable — like Kinfolk or Cereal magazine
- Camera references: Canon EOS R5, 35mm for rooms, 85mm for portraits

**Environments Sophie Appears In:**
- Her "studio" (bright, plants, mood boards on wall, fabric samples)
- Client homes (during "consultations")
- Furniture stores / showrooms
- Flea markets / thrift stores
- Cozy cafés (working on sketches)
- Hardware stores (picking paint samples)

**Wardrobe Rotation:**
1. Cream/oatmeal oversized knit sweater + jeans
2. White linen button-up + tan wide-leg pants
3. Sage green apron over white tee (when "working")
4. Denim jacket + simple dress (casual outings)
5. Cozy cardigan + reading glasses pushed up on head

## Competitive Positioning

Sophie is NOT:
- A luxury designer (she's accessible/budget-friendly)
- A minimalist extremist (she's warm and layered)
- A product review channel (products appear naturally in context)
- A real person pretending to be AI (transparency TBD based on platform strategy)

Sophie IS:
- The friend you wish you had when decorating your first apartment
- A bridge between "I have no idea what I'm doing" and "I love my space"
- A free personal designer (the "sale" is hidden inside genuine help)
